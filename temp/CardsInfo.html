Tables

Contents:

Intro

Tables are used to organise and arrange data and are important elements in many ABB applications. Complex tables can be challenging to design well and require particular design attention in each individual case.

About this chapter

Tables can be simple or complex. Table components (cells, rows) can affect their sibling components. Cells can affect their content and content can affect it's containers.

Semantics / Strucure

Tables typically contain a header at the top of the table followed by rows containing cells for holding data and interactive elements.

Cells

Cell types

Despite how complex and table may seem when it is fully populated with various content types, the variety of cell types is minimal. Cells are simply containers. It is their content that varys.

Cell design

Cell design is very simple - usually a border color, a background color and a padding setting. Sometimes a row's background color may change (see row & column tracking) giving the effect of the cells background color being changed due to opacity.

Cells tend to have simple styling properties so that they can be rendered on the screen, position their contents and set styles on standard/common content such as text or numbers.

Content alignment

Content alignment is a very important property of tables. Correct alignment allows user to scan and compare row and cell content.

In general:

  • Numbers : align right
  • Text : align left
  • Graphs and graphics : align center

Cell content types

Cell content

For the most part, cells are merely structured containers for various content types, with the sole purpose of holding that content in place so that user can easily find it and to verify the users mental model.

However, some elements can have slight style adjustments when placed inside a table cell. Buttons, for example, when placed inside a cell, expand to fill all the available space and lose their default border

img of a button and a button in a cell

How content is affected

Style adjustments are only meant to be surface deep. A dropdown menu placed within a table cell will also lose it's border and strecth to fill the space but when interacted with it's underlying styling will remain the same as if it were not in a cell.

Rows

Rows horizontally align cells. They usually have a minimal amount of styling applied (cells styling make up for most of the tables look and feel). Borders between rows can be a property of the row or of the cells. the outcome should be the same. Rows can also have states and be interactive.

Row styles - line types

There are two main line styles for rows:

  • Normal (subtle lines)
  • Normal (defined lines)

Use whichever style works best with your interface's background color.

Row styles - zebra stripes

Zebra styling is simply applying a background color to alternating rows in order to assist eye tracking.
